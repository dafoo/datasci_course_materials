Instructions for Setting up a Windows Server on AWS for Tableau Desktop
June 2013

1) Go to http://aws.amazon.com

2) Sign up for an account, if you don’t already have one

a. You will be asked to supply a credit card to have on hand, even though we will be using the free tier of AWS

b. Part of this verification process will be to receive a telephone call at the number you provide

c. The following screen serves as a confirmation of your enrollment

d. You will receive an email once your account is ready for use

3) Go to your “AWS Management Console” on the left sidebar once signed in, or just access at http://aws.amazon.com/console/


4) Click “Sign in to the AWS Console”

 


5) On the console home page, click “EC2”

 

6) Click “Launch Instance”

 

7) When prompted, go through the “Classic Wizard” and choose a “Microsoft Windows Server 2008 R2 Base” – this is a server covered under the free tier of AWS

8) Choose to create T1 Micro instance (it should default to this) and click continue

 

9) Click through the next page without making changes – click through the following page as well

 

10) You have the option of creating a key for this instance – this is optional metadata for your own purposes

 

11) Now we can create a key pair – give it a name and click “Create & Download your Key Pair.” Save this file ([keypairname].pem) somewhere convenient

 

12) Click through the next screen – but take note of the port mentioned on the right of the page (3389) 

 

13) Click “Launch” on the next page

 

14) You will receive a notification that your instance is launching – it will likely take several minutes for it to fully launch

 

15) Closing the previous screen will take you back to your list of AWS instances. Wait until your machine says “running” under state and “2/2 checks passed” under status checks. Then, right click the instance and click “connect”

 

16) On this screen, you’ll be given your credentials to log in to the virtual machine. Click “Retrieve Password” and upload your previously created private key to get your administrator password. Once you upload your key and get your password, take note of the login name (Administrator) and the password

a. For the next step, if you do not already have a remote desktop connection client, you can get the Microsoft Remote Desktop Connection Client for Mac or rdesktop for Unix-based distros

17) Open your remote desktop connection client and connect to your public IP address. Use your username and password previously provided to log in. Click through any security warnings that may arise

18) Your instance of Windows Server is up and running! Go to www.tableausoftware.com/tft/activation to download a copy of Tableau to this machine (you may have to click through some security notifications). Click “Get your copy of Tableau” and fill out the form provided

19) You will be redirected to a download page – if your download does not start immediately, click the “click here” text to start the download. If you receive a security pop-up, clicking “add” can help mitigate any problems you experience downloading the file

20) Open the file “TableauDesktop.exe” you’ve downloaded and click through the install wizard

21) Once the program installs, you will have three options to activate Tableau: choose “Activate the product”

22) Enter the product key provided

23) You will be asked to fill out your name and email address – please use the same information you used on the download page

24) Now you’re ready to start working with Tableau! To exit out of your AWS server, click the X in the upper middle of the screen – everything open will still be there when you connect to the server again

25) When you are eventually finished with your AWS server, back in your instances page, right click the instance and select “Terminate”
